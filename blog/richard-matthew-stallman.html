Richard Matthew Stallman

September 17, 2019 — Josh

Unfortunately, as of today 2019-16-09, Richard Stallman has resigned as president of the Free Software Foundation. I have to be honest, I don't agree with the outcome of this debacle. I am all for outing toxic people and removing them from communities, when deserved, but I believe that this situation was based on a complete misunderstanding of Stallman as a human being. As somebody who is exceptionally contrarian and pedantic, Stallman's behavior was completely characteristic of his typical self -- a true character, with good intentions but a persona which is definitely an acquired taste. I'm not attempting to glorify him. Understand, Stallman is not a normal person, he's not a millionaire business mogul, he's not a professor using his authority to manipulate his students into sleeping with him, he's not a predator -- he's an honest-to-god geek, hacker, nerd, creep straight out of a 70s/80s stereotype. On the political spectrum he's so far on the left that he may as well be off the grid -- I am sure he was mistaken as "another conservative male defending Epstein". On the Autism spectrum, he's likely present. A person with little to no social media exposure, no cell phone, ignorance to/of social rules, and who is a boomer has many things going against them when expected to conform to rapidly changing social standards.

It is obvious that these people have no idea who Stallman is. If you've visited his website, you may have read about Stallman's proposed alternative to the gender-neutral pronoun "they" -- "per". Stallman is seen using that pronoun above, but the author of the article went ahead and presumed it was a typo. What else did they presume?

My world is shaken. To me, rms is as influential as Dennis Ritchie or Ken Thompson (terribly sorry to bring their names into this). How will computer science live without Richard Stallman? I'm sorry, but I won't be removing Stallman from my homepage.
